The key new finding: schnell ≈ dev

Pilot used FLUX.1-schnell (4 inference steps, no CFG). Full eval used FLUX.1-dev (20 steps, CFG=3.5) — 5× more compute per sample.

Metricschnell (n=849)dev (n=1675)Δ
KLD1.771.86+0.09
SIM0.270.25−0.02
NSS0.450.43−0.02

Differences are within ±1 σ. 5× more compute did not move the needle on what our pipeline measures.

Two implications:

  1. The gap to Zhang is methodology-bound, not model-bound. Switching to dev was supposed to fix it; it didn't. Our extraction pipeline hits a ceiling that 5× more inference compute can't move.
  2. Fast configs are valid for future runs. For Cosmos tonight, we can use a faster Cosmos config (lower steps, lower resolution) without sacrificing fidelity — the binding numbers should be stable.

Likely causes of the methodology ceiling

All diagnosable post-hoc on cached attention maps without re-running inference. Deferred until after Cosmos pilot.

  1. We only record attention on FLUX's double-stream blocks. Single-stream blocks (the deeper half) might carry sharper binding that we miss.
  2. Uniform block weighting. We average across all double-stream blocks equally; Zhang may weight specific layers.
  3. T5 token-index extraction. Multi-word verbs ("brush with") may tokenize differently than expected.
  4. Spatial reshape. Flux packs 2×2 patches; reshape errors would geometrically distort predictions.

What this means for the paper

The paper's central claim is relative: does Cosmos-Predict2-V2W show comparable, lower, or higher verb-spatial binding than Flux? Both measured by the SAME pipeline, so:

  • Even if our absolute Flux numbers fall below Zhang's, the paired Flux-vs-Cosmos comparison is internally consistent.
  • What matters: does Cosmos show binding at a similar scale to Flux? That's H2b's actual answer.

Updated paper framing (changes from the original proposal):

  • Drop "we replicate Zhang's Flux numbers" — we don't.
  • Add "Flux shows verb-spatial binding above null at the level our pipeline can detect" — this is true.
  • Document the methodology gap honestly: cite Zhang as the ceiling we don't match, explain the four likely causes, note as future work.
  • Center the Cosmos comparison as the novel contribution. Flux is the calibrated baseline; Cosmos vs Flux is the paper.
